Spider-Man

Overview

Consistencies across different publications

The character of Spider-Man has appeared in many publications, beginning with comics in the 1960s and then going on to appear in animated and live-action series and films. Despite these many different iterations, he remains a distinct character with a consistent backstory and values.

Spider-Man is a young hero whose secret identity is Peter Parker, raised by his aunt May in Queens. He received superpowers from a radioactive spider bite. His powers include agility and a "spider-sense" and he uses a spiderweb material for combat and mobility. He wears a red costume which makes him recognizable while also concealing his identity.

Spider-Man's values are based on the concept, "With great power comes great responsibility," which is typically told to him by his Uncle Ben before he dies. These words, combined with Spider-Man's trauma/loss, push him to use his powers for good and protect the people he loves. Spider-Man often makes sacrifices in his personal life to prioritize his responsibility as a hero.
